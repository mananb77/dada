# **Dada (Kailash Prasad Bhargav) \- Life Story Summary**

## **Birth and Early Childhood (1941-1945)**

**Born:** February 13, 1941  
 **Birthplace:** Byawar, Rajasthan (180 kilometers from Jaipur)  
 **Neighborhood:** Diggi Mohalla

Dada was born into the prominent Bhargav family of Byawar. The family lived in Diggi Mohalla, in what was known as the "Bhargav Gali" (Bhargav Lane) because many members of the Bhargav family resided there. Their neighbors included Mahesh Datt Bhargav (who lived directly across from them), Kaushal Kishore Bhargav, and Ram Kishore Bhargav (Anil's father, not grandfather).

### **Earliest Memory**

Dada's earliest memory is from when he was approximately 4 years old. There was a platform (chabutra) in their house, about 2.5 feet high, inside a room with a window overlooking the neighborhood square (chowk). His younger brother Shambhu, who was about 2.5-3 years old at the time, could easily climb onto this platform, but Dada struggled to get up. This memory stands out as one of his first clear recollections of childhood.

### **Family Home Layout**

The family lived in a traditional haveli-style home:

* The main lane led into the house  
* Ground floor had tenants: Devilal, who worked in the court  
* Their family lived in the front room on the upper floor  
* Additional rooms were arranged in an L-shape  
* A small room (kothri) where Dada was born  
* A rooftop terrace where they kept pigeons and flew kites  
* A window in the chabutra room that overlooked the chowk (square)

## **The Partition Era (1947)**

### **August 15, 1947 \- Independence Day**

At age 6, Dada witnessed the traumatic events surrounding India's independence and partition. These memories left an indelible mark on his young mind.

### **The Train Massacre and Mor Singh's Intervention**

One of the most dramatic incidents Dada recalls involved:

**The Context:**

* Trains traveling between India and Pakistan were carrying refugees  
* Trains from Pakistan to India often arrived with dead bodies of Hindus \- men, women, and children who had been killed  
* Similar atrocities were happening to Muslims traveling the other direction

**The Incident at Kharwa:** A particularly significant event occurred near Kharwa (a place near Byawar):

**Mor Singh the Dacoit:**

* A dacoit (bandit) named Mor Singh operated in the Kharwa area  
* He lived under the protection of the Kharwa Thakur Sahab  
* When Mor Singh witnessed trains carrying dead Hindu bodies passing through, he decided to take action

**The Train Interception:**

* Mor Singh sent two of his men ahead on a train from Pakistan heading to Ajmer  
* These men positioned themselves with the driver  
* When the train reached Kharwa, they stopped it  
* All passengers were ordered off the train  
* Passengers were separated into two lines: Hindus on one side, Muslims on the other  
* Hindus were carefully identified to ensure no Muslims were hiding among them

**Om Bhai Sahab's Miraculous Escape:** Among the passengers was **Om Bhai Sahab** (son of Dada's eldest bua \- maternal aunt, and uncle to Dr. Gudda):

* He was traveling from Ajmer to Byawar  
* He had no janeu (sacred thread) or other obvious markers of being Hindu  
* Mor Singh called out to him in a loud voice: "Are you Hindu?"  
* Om Bhai Sahab confirmed: "Yes, I am Hindu"  
* "Where are you going?"  
* "I'm going to Dewan Bahadur Pyari Lal Ji's garden. My mama (maternal uncle) Mahesh Prasad Bhargav lives there"

**Mor Singh's Response:**

* "Go, go. I have eaten the salt of Dewan Bahadur Sahab (meaning: I am indebted to him and loyal to his family)"  
* "You can go safely, and I'm sending one of my men to escort you all the way to Byawar"

This phrase "I have eaten his salt" (maine unka namak khaya hai) signified a deep loyalty and obligation. Mor Singh's faithfulness to Dewan Bahadur Sahab saved Om Bhai Sahab's life.

### **The Anxious Wait at Home**

**Communication Challenges:**

* There were no telephones in most homes during this time  
* Dada's family had a neighbor, Mahesh Datt Bhargav, a lawyer who had a telephone  
* Their house had a small window (mokha) in the wall

**The Tense Hours:**

* Dada's father (Mahesh Prasad) repeatedly called through the small window to the lawyer  
* "Sahab, please telephone and find out if Om Ji's train has left or not"  
* Calls were made repeatedly to check on the train's status  
* After confirmation that the train had departed, Om Bhai Sahab still didn't arrive for 2 more hours  
* The family became extremely worried: "Where is he? What happened?"  
* News arrived that a train had been stopped at Kharwa  
* Relief came when they learned all Hindus were safe and coming on that same train  
* When Om Bhai Sahab finally reached home, there was immense relief and gratitude

### **Communal Violence in Byawar**

Dada also witnessed retaliatory violence in Byawar itself:

**The Chand Gate Incident:**

* Near Chand Gate, on the way to their family garden  
* There was a property owned by Arya Samajis (members of the Arya Samaj reform movement)  
* A Muslim man was traveling in a tonga (horse cart)  
* Hindu men, in retaliation for killings of Hindus, pulled him from the tonga  
* The Arya Samajis killed him on the spot  
* Their reasoning: "You are killing Hindus, so we will kill you"

These incidents deeply affected young Dada, showing him the brutal reality of communal violence during partition.

### **Life During Curfew**

* Curfew was imposed in Byawar during this period  
* The family stayed indoors for safety  
* Communication was difficult and dangerous  
* The small window (mokha) in their wall became their connection to the outside world

## **The Daily Walk to College (1961-1963)**

### **The Incredible Commute**

One of the most remarkable aspects of Dada's student life in Jaipur was his daily journey to Commerce College:

**Distance:** 7 kilometers each way \= **15 kilometers per day total** (approximately 9-10 miles)

**Route:**

* Started from Jaswant Singh Ji's house  
* Walked through Bhatta area  
* Passed by Harwa Mahal  
* Continued to Commerce College

**Why Walking?** **Financial Necessity:**

* Monthly income/stipend: Only **₹30**  
* Bus fare: **4 annas** (one way)  
* To save money, Dada chose to walk entirely

**His Philosophy:**

* When people asked, "Why don't you take the bus?"  
* Dada replied: "Bhai, exercise ho jati hai meri" (Brother, I get my exercise this way)  
* In reality: "₹30 mein mujhe sara saal nikalna hai" (I have to manage the whole year on ₹30)

**The Math:**

* ₹30 per month was his entire budget  
* Bus fare would have consumed a significant portion  
* Walking saved approximately ₹8 per month (assuming 2 trips x 4 annas x 30 days)  
* This 25-30% savings was crucial for survival

### **Physical Demands**

**Daily Walking:**

* 7 km to college in the morning  
* 7 km back home in the evening  
* Additional 1 km moving around campus  
* Total: **15 kilometers per day**  
* This equals approximately **10 miles of walking daily**  
* Maintained this routine for several months

**Weather Conditions:**

* Jaipur's extreme temperatures: very hot summers, cold winters  
* No protection from sun or rain  
* Walked in all weather conditions  
* Built remarkable stamina and endurance

### **The Bicycle Era**

**After 2-3 Months:**

* Dada went back to Byawar for a visit

* This made the commute much easier  
* Cycling 14 km was still significant but much better than walking

**Significance:**

* The bicycle represented a major improvement in quality of life

* A valuable skill for secretarial and administrative work
