## Mahesh Prasad Bhargava

## About This Document

*This document preserves the stories, reflections, and lessons shared by the family during the group Zoom call held in the days following Dada's passing. It draws on both the call transcript and Dada's own recorded life story interview.*

Dada left this world approximately 26 days after Mami, on a Wednesday in February 2025. This document is intended to be a living tribute — a reference for the family, and especially for the next generation, so that his memory and teachings endure.

---

## Who Was Dada — A Brief Biography

### Early Life & Origins

- Born on **13 February 1941** in Byavar (near Ajmer, Rajasthan) — a town about 180 km from Jaipur
- Grew up in **Diggi Mohalla**, a tight-knit neighbourhood of Bhargava families
- Neighbours included Mahesh Dutt Bhargava, Kaushil Kishore Bhargava, Ram Kishore Bhargava — families that would remain connected for life

### Witnessing Partition (1947)

At just six years old, Dada witnessed the trauma of India's Partition firsthand. Trains arriving from Pakistan carried bodies. A local dacoit named Mor Singh lived near their home, and it was he who helped protect the family — vouching for who was Hindu and who was Muslim, and safely escorting people to safety.

> *"He absorbed the weight of history at an age when most children are simply playing kite on the roof."*

### The Self-Made Man

- Sold candles and took on whatever work was needed in his early years
- Worked his way through education and into the banking sector
- Served in Jaipur Bank and later Union Bank — famous for being posted to loss-making branches and turning them profitable
- Colleagues said: when Bhargava Sahib was transferred in, the branch came back to life
- After retirement, started his own business ventures — something most of his peers only talked about doing

### Post-Retirement Entrepreneurship

Many people say "when I retire, I'll do something." Dada actually did it. While peers dreamed of business, he launched one. He explored astrology, homeopathy, and multiple streams of income — not out of financial need, but out of a deep-rooted drive to keep contributing.

> *"Kailash mama was the only one among all the people I know who actually started something after retirement. Everyone else just talked about it."*
> — Sonu (on the call)

### Health Journey

- Underwent a TAVR (heart) procedure in 2017
- Was signing business cheques in the ICU the very next day
- The attending cardiologist, Dr. Ravindra Rao, reportedly photographed him doing paperwork as a testament to his procedure's success
- Made business calls even on the Wednesday he passed away — entrepreneurial until his very last hours

### His Bond with Mami

Their love was a force. Mami passed away on the 6th of February, and Dada followed her approximately 26 days later. Family members felt that Mami had been Dada's spiritual shield — his "armor" — and that once she was gone, nothing held him here.

> *"Mami used to say: 'Mujhe suhaagan hi jaana hai — mere suhag ko kuch nahi hoga.' She was right. He couldn't survive long without her."*
> — Pallav on the call

> *"Saat janam ka bandhan tha. Ek gaye, doosre bhi gaye."*
> — Family sentiment shared by multiple members

## His Character & Defining Qualities

### 1. Physical Stamina & Wellness Philosophy

*Dada placed extraordinary emphasis on the body as the foundation for everything else. He practised what he preached — maintaining energy levels that outpaced men decades younger.*

- Insisted from childhood: *"Ek litre doodh pi liya karo"* (drink a litre of milk daily)
- Advocated sattoo, badam (almonds), morning walks, and light exercise as non-negotiable
- Well into his seventies, could outwork people in their thirties in physical labour
- Drove cars at high speed well into old age — family members laughingly recalled gripping the door

> *"It all comes back to stamina. Build it young, and it carries you far. That's what Dada showed us — even at 80, his mind was healthy because his body was cared for."*
> — Sonu on the call

### 2. Undivided Attention & Presence

*Perhaps the most universal memory shared on the call: when Dada spoke with you, you had his complete attention. Nothing else in the room existed.*

- Whether on a brief call or a 30-minute conversation, he was fully present
- Never seemed distracted, never made you feel like an interruption
- Even in hospital post-surgery, he greeted visitors by asking about their trip first

> *"The entire concentration was just on you. Nothing in that moment mattered but you."*
> — Ila on the call

> *"He was tired, he had just had surgery — but the first thing he asked me was 'beta, tera trip kaisa ja raha hai?'"*
> — Ila on the call

### 3. Human Connection Across Every Level

*Dada treated every person with equal dignity and genuine curiosity — from hospital labour staff to government ministers.*

- Always asked names — and remembered them
- In hospital, would ask ward boys their village, their family, their story — and connect on that level
- Labour workers reported that he was the one sahib who treated them like humans
- Respected the gardener as much as the general manager

> *"Jo bhi log mile, chahe hospital ka staff ho ya koi bhi — sab se naam poochte the. Aur phir aise connect ho jaate the jaise unhi ke gaon ke hain."*
> — Harsh (son) on the call

### 4. Extraordinary Memory & Knowledge

*His mental sharpness was legendary in the family.*

- Remembered names of second and third cousins, Bhargavas across India and abroad, for decades
- Knew exact addresses, histories, and connections across the Bhargava community spanning generations
- Taught himself astrology and homeopathy in the gaps between his professional life
- Was a knowledge resource on topics from medicine to real estate to family genealogy

> *"Aap kisi bhi field mein poochhein — he can guide you. I don't know how he retained all of it."*

### 5. Entrepreneurial Spirit & Work Ethic

*He never stopped. Even in retirement, when most rest, Dada expanded.*

- Reinvented himself post-banking — started businesses when peers sat idle
- Made business calls the morning he passed away, and on days he was in hospital
- Signed business cheques in the ICU after open-heart surgery
- Could multitask across personal, business, and community matters simultaneously

> *"I don't know where the fuel was coming from. Subah se raat tak — he could do different things without stopping."*
> — Pallav on the call

### 6. Selfless Generosity

*He gave freely — money, time, energy, connections — to anyone who needed it.*

- Covered medical bills for household staff when costs ran into lakhs, without hesitation
- Once helped an accident victim on the highway — called an ambulance, managed the scene, sent him to hospital — all without being asked
- Lent money to people even when his own liquidity was uncertain
- When his son was going to the USA, gave him 101 names and said: *"Ye sab tujhe help kar denge"*

> *"Uski insaniyat mein sabko saath leke chalna tha. Family, Bhargava community, and anyone who came through the door."*

### 7. The Great Communicator

*He was, by every account, a master of persuasion, warmth, and storytelling.*

- Could charm anyone with sweet, thoughtful words — *"Meeta bolta tha"* was the universal observation
- Made people feel important the moment they spoke with him
- Could convince his family to stay four more years in the country; could also convince a nurse to return his mobile phone
- Loved pulling pranks — pretended to be a stranger on calls; once nearly had a family member drive to a fake property showing

> *"Aaj tum bahut achhe lag rahe ho — he would say something like that. And you would respond in kind. That was his talent."*
> — Pallav on the call

## Lessons & Teachings

*The family distilled his life into the following lessons, shared organically during the remembrance call.*

### On Health & Longevity

> *"Ek litre doodh pi, sattoo khao, badam khao — and build your stamina young. You'll need it for a long innings."*
> — Dada's consistent advice

- Physical discipline is not optional — it is the foundation of everything else
- A healthy mind runs on a healthy body; his mental clarity into his eighties was proof
- Morning walks, consistent routine, and nutrition are not trends — they are wisdom

### On Work & Purpose

> *"Don't wait for retirement to do the thing you want to do. Do it now. He did."*
> — Family reflection

- Work is not a burden — it is an expression of vitality
- The goal is not to retire *from* something, but to keep *building* something
- Learn new things continuously: astrology, homeopathy, real estate, finance — all in the margins of a busy life

### On Relationships & Community

> *"Agar Bhargava hai, toh apna hi hai. Jao aur mil lo."*
> — Dada's instinct whenever he heard of a Bhargava nearby

- The social fabric your elders weave — you inherit it. But you must also weave your own.
- Call people. Visit people. Return calls. Connections die when you stop tending them.
- Every level of society deserves your respect and full presence.
- Help first, ask questions later — there will always be enough.

### On Family

> *"Apni family ko saath leke chalo. Apni community ko saath leke chalo."*
> — Dada's consistent message

- Family is not just blood — it is built through consistent acts of love over decades
- Walk in your parents' footsteps — not blindly, but carefully, picking the best of what they gave
- The next generation learns not from what you say, but from what you do in front of them

### On Attitude Toward Life

> *"Khush hokey rehna hai yahan. Khush hokey jaana."*
> — Dada's words to his son, 48 hours before passing

- He never complained about pain or illness in front of others
- He made difficult situations lighter — his hospital room always felt less heavy when he was in it
- Step by step — the same patience he showed teaching a toddler to climb stairs applies to all of life

## Memorable Stories

### The Property Prank

Sonu called Dada pretending to be a real estate agent, saying there was land available for sale in Kanota. Dada had no idea it was a prank. He was so engaged — asking about the property in detail — that he had already begun moving toward his car to go and see it before Sonu revealed who she really was.

> *"Meri bahut hi mazedaar yaadon mein se hai yeh. He was so ready to go — that was Dada."*
> — Sonu on the call

### The Hospital Phone Standoff

After his TAVR procedure, a junior ward staff member refused to give Dada his mobile phone, saying it would drain his energy. Dada, undeterred, quietly mobilised his network: he got a message out via the milk-delivery boy's son, called his daughter-in-law, asked her to bring "Coco" (a favourite companion), and eventually had Dr. Rao himself intervene to restore his phone. He did not rest until he had it back.

> *"Mujhe phone nahi de rahe — mujhse itne tameez se baat nahi ki jaise kisi insaan se nahi ki. Abhi ke abhi aao."*
> — Dada to his son, at 2 AM

### Signing Cheques in the ICU

The day after his surgery, Dada was signing business cheques for the hostel from his hospital bed. When Dr. Ravindra Rao walked in and saw this, he took out his phone and photographed it — saying this was exactly the kind of recovery proof he wanted to show for his procedure.

### Teaching Anish to Climb Stairs

When Anish (grandson) was barely able to walk, Dada spent 15-minute sessions teaching him to climb stairs at Pramod Chacha's house — stairs that had no railing on one side. He came down to the child's level, used only actions and sounds (not words, since Anish couldn't yet speak), showed each step patiently, and once Anish had it, the child was unstoppable — running up and down every staircase he could find.

> *"It was like watching someone teach not with words, but with love and patience. He connected through action and emotion. That stayed with Anish."*

### The Mehandipur Balaji Pilgrimages

On Sunday mornings, out of nowhere, a call would come: Dada was on his way, and everyone had 15-20 minutes to get ready. Half an hour later, the whole family would be in the car heading to Mehandipur Balaji or some other destination. No planning, no agenda — just joy and movement.

> *"That was his way of doing family time. Not scheduled, not formal — just 'chalo, chal rahe hain.'"*

### The Trimbakeshwar Trip with Bahu

After his TAVR recovery, one of Dada's wishes was to visit Trimbakeshwar. He invited his daughter-in-law to join him, stayed at the Taj Hotel in Bombay, negotiated shrines at Shirdi (Sai Baba) and Shani Maharaj into the itinerary — all under the same cab arrangement — and called the entire adventure *"advertising for Dr. Rao,"* since he sent so many people to the doctor afterward.

## His Final Days

*The family pieced together what Dada knew — or felt — in his last 48 hours.*

### 48 Hours Before

- Organised Buaji's life story to be recorded at the dining table, with a microphone
- Was checking: *"Mami ka kaam sab achhe se ho gaya? Koi kami to nahi rahi?"* — making sure every ritual for Mami had been completed properly
- Set aside two prasad boxes — one for Anisha, one for Vedang — to be taken back to the US, so the grandchildren received Mami's prasad
- Told his son: *"Khush hokey jaana. Khush hokey rehna hai yahan."*

### The Morning He Left

- Made business calls that morning
- Dr. Sanjiv checked his vitals at 3 PM — all normal; declared fit and fine
- Called his son through Bhai Ji approximately 2.5 hours before passing to ask him to stay 15 more days
- Said: *"Tujhe rook ke hi chhodunga"* — I will send you only after I've made you stay longer
- Left at approximately **7:20 PM**

### The Spiritual Reading

Family members reflected that Mami had been Dada's impenetrable spiritual armor. In 2017, when Dada had his cardiac event, Mami had said: *"Mera suhag safe hai."* He survived. Once Mami was gone, that shield was gone too. He had his own great store of good deeds (sat-karm), but the spiritual protection was hers.

> *"He was living because of Mami. When Mami went, that armor was gone. He had 26 more days — and that's how he lived them."*
> — Shailendra (Binu) on the call

## His Legacy

### The Social Fabric

Dada spent a lifetime building a network that now spans continents. Bhargavas in Chicago, Atlanta, Toronto, Jaipur, Udaipur — connected because of him. Every time a family member moved to a new city, he gave them names. That network is a living gift.

> *"We take it for granted — 'of course I can call so-and-so.' But not every family has this. Dada created it. We must now weave our own section of that fabric."*
> — Pallav on the call

### What He Leaves Behind

- A family that knows how to show up for each other
- A model of entrepreneurship, learning, and never stopping
- The demonstration that stamina built young sustains you for a lifetime
- The proof that every person deserves your full presence, not a distracted half-attention
- The lesson that love — for spouse, children, grandchildren, community — is a verb, not a feeling

### The Standard He Set

> *"He has set a standard for us. Itna toh kiya hi ja sakta hai. We have seen it. When we fall short, we can always think: 'he did this. So can we.'"*
> — Rajat, close of call
